# Supply Chain Business Insights & Recommendations

**Project:** Supply Chain Analytics - DataCo Global (180,511 orders, 2015-2017)  
**Analyst:** Aswathy Chandrasekar  
---

## Critical Issues

### 1. Late Delivery Risk is Systemic (55%)
**Finding:** 55% of all orders are at risk of late delivery across ALL markets equally.  
**Implication:** This is not a regional logistics problem - it is a company-wide operational failure.  
**Recommendation:**
- Audit the order fulfillment process end-to-end
- Investigate warehouse processing times as the root cause
- Set a KPI target: reduce late delivery risk below 30% within 2 quarters

---

### 2. First Class Shipping is Paradoxically the Worst (95% Late Risk)
**Finding:** First Class shipping - the premium option - has the highest late delivery risk at 95%, compared to Standard Class at 38%.  
**Implication:** Customers paying more for faster delivery are being let down the most. This is a major churn and trust risk.  
**Recommendation:**
- Immediately investigate First Class fulfillment SLAs with logistics partners
- Consider suspending First Class until the issue is resolved
- Proactively communicate with affected customers

---

## Revenue Opportunities

### 3. Fishing Category is Underserved (High Profit, Low Volume)
**Finding:** Fishing is the #1 profit category ($756K) but only ranks 7th in units sold (17K).  
**Implication:** High margin product with untapped demand potential.  
**Recommendation:**
- Increase marketing spend on Fishing category
- Expand product range within Fishing
- Bundle Fishing products with Camping & Hiking (also high profit) for cross-sell

---

### 4. Fan Shop Concentration Risk (47% of Revenue)
**Finding:** Fan Shop drives $17.1M - nearly half of all revenue.  
**Implication:** Over-reliance on a single department is a major business risk. Any disruption to Fan Shop (supply issues, demand shift) would critically impact the business.  
**Recommendation:**
- Diversify revenue by investing in Apparel ($7.9M) and Golf ($4.6M)
- Set a strategic target: no single department should exceed 35% of revenue

---

### 5. Discount Strategy is Eroding Profit ($3.7M Discounts vs $3.93M Profit)
**Finding:** Total discounts given ($3.7M) nearly match total profit ($3.93M).  
**Implication:** The business is essentially giving away its profit margin through discounts.  
**Recommendation:**
- Implement discount caps by category (max 8% for high-margin, 5% for low-margin)
- Run A/B tests to measure discount impact on conversion vs margin
- Focus discounts only on categories with excess inventory

---

## Market Strategy

### 6. Africa is an Untapped Growth Market
**Finding:** Africa generates the least revenue ($2.3M) but has the same profit margin (~22%) as Europe ($10M+).  
**Implication:** Africa is profitable but underpenetrated - not a losing market, just an underinvested one.  
**Recommendation:**
- Increase logistics and marketing investment in Africa
- Focus on Standard Class shipping (lowest late risk at 38%) for African markets initially

---

### 7. Consumer Segment Dominates but Corporate Has Higher Efficiency
**Finding:** Consumer segment has 10,694 customers and $19M revenue, but Corporate segment generates $183 avg sales per customer vs Consumer's $183 - nearly identical.  
**Implication:** Corporate clients are equally valuable per customer but far fewer in number.  
**Recommendation:**
- Launch a B2B acquisition program targeting Corporate clients
- Offer Corporate-specific shipping SLAs and account management

---

## Forecast Summary

Based on the Prophet forecasting model:
- **Predicted monthly revenue (H1 2018):** $872K-$952K
- **January/February dip** is seasonal and expected
- **Revenue trend is stable** - no significant growth without strategic intervention

**To grow beyond $1M/month consistently:**
1. Fix the late delivery crisis (retain existing customers)
2. Expand Fishing & Camping categories (margin improvement)
3. Reduce discount dependency (profit protection)
4. Invest in Africa & Corporate segments (new revenue streams)
